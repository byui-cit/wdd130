Your Personal Site Content

This week we will do an activity to help us generate the content we will need to complete our personal site.

Requirements

  1. The Web site must consist of a home page and at least 2 child pages
  2. One of the child pages should incorporate a form.
  3. The Web site must be hosted on the web.
  4. Each page of your Web site must satisfy the following requirements:
    1. Significant textual content consisting of appropriate content for your audience so that they can understand the meaning and message of this site
    2. At least one image that relates to the content of the page.
    3. Working links to your two child pages.
    4. Be laid out and styled according to your site plan.
    5. Be visually appealing and easily readable.

Activity Directions

  1. Brainstorm

    Over that last couple of weeks you were asked to decide on a topic for your personal website and create an elevator pitch "selling" your idea. You should also have thought about your intended audience. This week, begin by reviewing your pitch and audience, then think about three topics your site will cover. Write those three topics down.

  2. Write!

    With your topics and audience in mind, please write at least 600 words on your website idea providing text content that your audience may want to read or get from your website. You can just free-write any pertinent information about your website idea following good paragraph and sentence structure, thought processes, etc. This content will be a rough draft this week, but will be refined in subsequent weeks to become what the users will be reading from your website. You may also consider answering some or all of the following questions about your website idea:
    • Why will people come to your site?
    • What questions will your visitors expect your site to answer for them?
    • What types of activities will visitors want to complete on your site?
    • What information is necessary to complete any actions your site offers?
    • Why will your visitors come to your site instead of another to get this information or complete the actions?

  3. Organize

    1. Organize your paragraphs so there is "one idea per paragraph". This may involve breaking up or otherwise editing paragraphs.
    2. Name each paragraph by typing and bolding a paragraph-topic name for each paragraph. This process can help you determine link names and additional topics that will be in your website.
    3. Organize your paragraphs by collecting and moving all the paragraphs that are "related" and "alike" into respective areas along with their paragraph-topic names. For example, you might have multiple paragraphs in your document pertaining to benefits of your website idea. You should move all of those paragraphs (leaving the paragraph name) into the same area of your document.
    4. Finally, consider each set of related paragraphs. Identify a representative "main-topic" title for each group. Each of these groups could become a page on your site. The "main-topic" label you choose would become the link text for the page in the main navigation. Remember that for our site we will have at least three pages. One of which will be a form. So you should pick at 2–3 of your main-topics that you will use for your site. One for the home page, and one for a child page, and possibly one for the page with a form (the form can either be used to complete an action: order, post, update, etc or to contact the owner of the site).

  4. Images

    Images play a very large role in how we view and feel about websites. You should put some thought into the images you will need to back up and enhance the content you generated above. To begin watch this short video

    For each of your pages find 2–3 images that could be used to enhance the content of that page. These can be images that you create yourself, or images that you have found. If you find the images online make sure to honor copyright. Do not use images that are not stated as free to use. Here are a couple of good sources for good, high quality, free stock images and icons.

    Add your chosen images to your siteplan for each page.

  5. Update site-plan

    Update the content section of your site plan with the "main-topic" names you have chosen. Add the paragraph titles and text for each section.
